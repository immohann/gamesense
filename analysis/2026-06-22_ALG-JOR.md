## PHASE 2 — GAME FLOW MAP

**1. What each team needs:**
| Team | Situation | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Algeria | 0pts, MUST WIN | Loss = 0pts vs Austria in MD3 (controllable but desperate). Win = 3pts. |
| Jordan | 0pts, MUST WIN | Loss = 0pts vs Argentina rotation in MD3 = certain elimination. Win = alive. |

**Both teams must win tonight → this is the dominant variable.** Neither team can afford to defend for a draw. Game is triggered from kick-off. Pattern 2 applies in REVERSE: the same group table math that made ARG-AUT an Under (neither needed to win) makes this an Over (both must win, both attack).

**2. Tactical shape:**
Both teams line up in **mirror 3-4-2-1**. This creates a key dynamic: the wide channels between each side's wingbacks become the battleground. Algeria's wingbacks (Ait-Nouri left, Belghali right) are offensively superior. When Jordan must also push their wingbacks forward to attack, they expose the three CBs to Algeria's wide overloads.

**3. Scoring mechanisms:**

*Algeria:*
- **Primary:** Mahrez (confirmed starting) cutting inside from right half-space onto left foot. Against Jordan's three CBs, one must come out to press — creating space for Gouiri's late run from behind.
- **Secondary:** Ait-Nouri overlapping left — wide crosses into the box with three central runners (Maza central, Gouiri + Mahrez arriving).
- **Set pieces:** Mahrez quality delivery. Jordan allowed OG + pen vs Austria = fragile at dead balls.

*Jordan:*
- **Primary:** Tamari pace on the right, roaming forward. RotoWire names him as Jordan's standout. Algeria WILL overcommit (must win pressure), creating counter-attack channels.
- **Secondary:** Olwan scored vs Austria. Movement between Algeria's backline on transitions.
- **Mechanism requires:** Algeria pushing men forward and leaving space behind their wingbacks. This is guaranteed given Algeria's must-win position.

**4. Jordan's defensive plan:**
Jordan's strength is their 3-4-2-1 low block + counter. But tonight they cannot hold that shape — they must attack. Their best defensive formation becomes unavailable. A Jordan chasing a goal is Jordan at their worst shape defensively. The one thing that makes them competitive (disciplined compact defense) is structurally removed by the group table.

**5. When does the game open:**
Open from kick-off. No settling period. First goal accelerates further — the conceding team immediately intensifies their attack, creating more space and more goals. This accelerates throughout the second half.

**Key unknown:** Can Algeria break down Jordan's relatively compact three-man defense? Mahrez confirmed starting answers this partially. The real question is whether Jordan's wingbacks hold their defensive shape or fully commit to attack — if they go all-in on offense, Algeria's wide overloads become devastating.

## PHASE 4 — STRESS TEST

**Flaw 1: Eimer's Under 2.5 (the sharpest dissent)**
- Eimer's mechanism for Under: "desperate teams play tight" / cagey first half from fear of losing.
- Counter-mechanism: Group table math says BOTH must win. Neither can afford to park the bus for even 30 minutes. This is the explicit inverse of ARG-AUT (Pattern 2 in reverse). Eimer may be anchoring on "desperation = caution" without running the table math: both teams' group position forces them to attack from minute 1.
- **Verdict: Eimer's Under is rejected.** The mechanism for Under requires one team to defend for a draw — and neither team's table position permits it. Over 2.5 confidence stays High.

**Flaw 2: Algeria's xG (0.1-0.2 vs ARG) → mechanism broken?**
- The 0-shots-on-target was against the best CB pair in the tournament. Jordan's backline is NOT Romero/Lisandro.
- Mahrez is confirmed starting — the primary creative mechanism is active.
- Quality adjustment mandatory: Algeria's attack is not what their MD1 xG shows.
- **Impact: Algeria winner call stays Medium (not dropped). Cannot be High because we haven't seen what this Algeria team does in full attack mode yet — but the adjustment is clear.**

**Flaw 3: Jordan's 1-3 scoreline overstates their defensive problems**
- 4 shots on target, scored to make it 1-1 at 50'. They were competitive against Austria.
- The 3-1 result was largely an OG + stoppage pen — not sustained attacking from Austria.
- **Impact: BTTS mechanism is REAL. Jordan scoring tonight is not a surprise — Olwan scored vs AUT, Tamari is their standout, Algeria WILL leave counter space.** BTTS Yes confidence stays High.

**Flaw 4: FOX Sports' "Algeria win to nil" (+140)**
- Requires Jordan NOT to score. But: Jordan's underlying xG vs AUT was 0.9-1.2. Olwan scored. Tamari is dangerous on the counter. Algeria pushing men forward WILL create counter chances.
- **Verdict: Avoid "Algeria win to nil." BTTS mechanism overrides clean sheet call.** This is where FOX's pick breaks — they're anchoring on Algeria's quality without modeling Jordan's counter threat.

**Flaw 5: Mirror formation — Algeria's attack may be less dominant than expected**
- Both in 3-4-2-1. Midfield congestion in center means Algeria can't just overpower with numbers.
- BUT: Algeria's wingbacks (Ait-Nouri particularly) are individually superior to Jordan's. Width is where Algeria win this.
- **Impact: Medium (not High) confidence on Algeria winner. The mirror formation means this is more competitive than ML -195 implies. Don't back Algeria -1.5 AH.**

**Flaw 6: Chaibi Racing Post pick — NOT in starting lineup**
- Racing Post's bet builder specifically picks Chaibi anytime to score. Chaibi is not in the confirmed starting 11.
- If Racing Post's specialist knows Chaibi comes off the bench and impacts games in the second half, it's still a valid impact sub pick — but not an anytime scorer first-choice pick.
- **Impact: Don't back Chaibi as primary scorer. Gouiri (confirmed starter, primary cluster pick at +125) is the correct play.**
