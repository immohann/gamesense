# 🇳🇿 NEW ZEALAND
**FIFA Ranking:** 85th | **Coach:** Darren Bazeley (English, ex-Watford/Wolves player) | **System:** 4-2-3-1 | **Group:** G

---

## Identity & DNA

- **Playing style:** Direct, physical, set-piece reliant. Not a possession team. Press high when motivated, absorb and counter when defending a result. Bazeley built a structured, disciplined shape that prioritizes defensive compactness first.
- **Under pressure:** Showed genuine resilience vs Iran — came back from behind, scored twice including a second-half goal. Not a team that folds. The 2010 World Cup DNA (three draws, unbeaten) lives in the culture — they believe they can hold anyone.
- **Set pieces:** Primary attacking mechanism. Chris Wood holds up, Elijah Just runs beyond. Joe Bell delivers corners and free kicks. Corners and free kicks are where NZL score most goals — NOT open play combinations. **TOURNAMENT CONFIRMED: Bell corner → Surman header at 15' vs Egypt. Set pieces fire from minute 1, not just second half.**
- **Tempo:** Slow start, physical and direct. Not a technical team. Will look long early and settle into the game. Second half tends to be more open as they commit.
- **Opponent style sensitivity (CRITICAL):** Against possession/compact teams (Egypt-style, Spain-style), their open-play creation nearly disappears — almost zero transition chances when the opponent controls the ball and defends deep. Against pressing/direct teams (Iran-style, Uruguay-style), the game is more open and NZL get more transition opportunities. This determines BTTS and Over/Under calls more than any other variable.
- **Collapse risk:** When forced to chase a deficit, NZL's shape breaks and they become badly exposed on the counter. Confirmed vs Egypt: led 1-0 HT, conceded three in the second half as they had to open up.

## Betting Patterns

- **Over/Under tendency:** Over — both WC matches produced goals, and NZL concede in most competitive matches. BUT total goals are heavily opponent-style dependent. Against possession/compact teams (Egypt), their open-play creation nearly disappears and the Over depends entirely on set pieces.
- **BTTS tendency:** Yes — conceded in every competitive match. But BTTS Yes requires NZL to also score, which is NOT guaranteed vs compact opponents who don't give up transition space. Set pieces are their only reliable scoring route vs organized defenses.
- **First half vs second half:** **CRITICAL PATTERN: Set-piece danger exists from MINUTE 1** (scored at 7' vs Iran, 15' vs Egypt). Do not conflate "slow tempo team" with "won't score in 1H." However, NZL collapse badly in second halves when chasing deficits.
- **Scorer watch:** Bell corner delivery → Surman/Wood aerial = the confirmed tournament mechanism. Just is the open-play threat but failed vs compact Egypt. Against organized defenses, look at Surman/Wood for set-piece scorers, not Just.
- **⚠️ Key watch:** When NZL fall behind, never back them to keep it close — shape breaks, more goals follow. Never back NZL to keep a clean sheet (conceded in every competitive match). Set-piece mechanism is NOT tempo-dependent — back it regardless of perceived tempo.
