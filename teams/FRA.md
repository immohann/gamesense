# 🇫🇷 FRANCE
**FIFA Ranking:** 2nd | **Coach:** Didier Deschamps | **System:** 4-2-3-1 | **Group:** I

---

## Identity & DNA

- **Playing style:** Tactically flexible but built around Mbappe as the central attacking mechanism. Deschamps sets up with a compact midfield two (Tchouameni/Rabiot/Kone rotation), Olise as creative #10, and wide pace (Dembele on right, Barcola/Thuram on left). **The key tactical discovery from MD1: Olise playing centrally (NOT wide) is France's best attacking shape.** It took a halftime switch to unlock it vs Senegal — it must now be the starting setup.
- **First half pattern — historical weakness:** France created just 0.02 xG in the first half vs Senegal (54% possession, almost no chances). This is a recurring first-half flatness across multiple internationals. The halftime Olise adjustment produced 1.87 xG in 45 minutes and three goals. The critical open question: has Deschamps institutionalized the Olise-centrally formula from kickoff, or does it still require a halftime adjustment?
- **Defensive vulnerability:** Failed to keep a clean sheet in 6 consecutive matches heading into this tournament. Conceded to Senegal's Mbaye in stoppage time (90+5'). France's transition defense when Dembele and Barcola push forward creates pockets of space in behind that pace-on-the-counter teams exploit. This is a structural weakness, not a one-off.
- **Rotation policy:** Deschamps rotates significantly vs weaker opponents. Barcola, Digne, Kone all expected to start vs Iraq in place of Thuram, Hernandez, Tchouameni. The quality barely drops — France's bench could start for most international teams.
- **Sub quality:** Doue, Thuram, Cherki, Mateta, Camavinga — among the most dangerous attacking subs in the tournament. France's depth is genuinely elite.

## Key Players

- **Kylian Mbappe** — Real Madrid — Captain / CF — All-time France top scorer (surpassed Giroud vs Senegal). Most World Cup goals for France (surpassed Fontaine vs Senegal). 14 WC goals total, chasing Klose's all-time record of 16. Penalty taker. The entire French attacking system terminates at Mbappe — Olise threads the through-ball, Mbappe drives at defenders with his exceptional pace. **Every attacking route ends at Mbappe. If he is even slightly below 100%, France's ceiling drops significantly.**
- **Michael Olise** — Bayern Munich — AM / #10 (centrally) — The tactical revelation of France's tournament. When played centrally, he transforms France's attacking output. When played wide, France are flat (0.02 xG in 45 minutes vs Senegal). His through-balls to Mbappe's runs behind the defensive line are France's primary scoring mechanism. His starting position (central vs wide) is THE single most important tactical variable to track before any France match.
- **Bradley Barcola** — PSG — Left Wing — Scored on WC debut in just 131 seconds coming off the bench vs Senegal (82'). Explosive direct runner, dangerous in behind. Now pushing to start. If he starts over Thuram, France have more directness and pace on the left.
- **Ousmane Dembele** — PSG — Right Wing — Creative wide outlet, generates assists and creates overloads through dribbling. Enables Mbappe and Olise through the middle by pulling defensive attention wide.
- **Adrien Rabiot** — Free agent / Marseille — Central Mid — Consistent screen and press. Regular starter. Reliable if unspectacular.
- **Manu Kone** — Real Madrid — Central Mid — Rotational option for Tchouameni. Box-to-box dynamism.
- **Aurelien Tchouameni** — Real Madrid — Central Mid / anchor — The defensive pivot when Deschamps wants more defensive security. Rested vs Iraq.
- **William Saliba** — Arsenal — CB — Elite ball-player at the back. Playing through pain but expected to continue. One of the best CBs in the tournament.
- **Dayot Upamecano** — Bayern Munich — CB — Physical, aerial. Can be caught in behind by high-pace attackers. France's slight defensive weak link.
- **Mike Maignan** — AC Milan — GK — World-class shot-stopper. Has not been heavily tested in the tournament so far given France's dominance.
- **Sub quality (entire bench):** Doue, Thuram, Cherki, Mateta, Camavinga, Gusto — this bench could start for most national teams. France's depth is the best in the tournament.
