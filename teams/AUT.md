# 🇦🇹 AUSTRIA
**FIFA Ranking:** 26th | **Coach:** Ralf Rangnick | **System:** 4-2-3-1 / gegenpressing | **Group:** J

---

## Identity & DNA

- **Playing style:** Rangnick's signature gegenpressing — high press, win the ball back quickly in advanced areas, transition fast. Austria do not sit off opponents. They press high and look to win possession in dangerous areas and immediately attack.
- **Baumgartner absence (CRITICAL):** Christoph Baumgartner is OUT for the tournament with injury. He was Austria's #10 — the player who creates through-balls in tight spaces, unlocks deep blocks, and connects the press to the attack. Without him, Austria's creative ceiling against organized defenses drops significantly. Sabitzer and Schmid share his creative duties but are not direct replacements.
- **Under pressure:** Beat Jordan 3-1 in MD1 but the scoreline flatters them. Needed an own goal (76') and a 90+12' penalty to seal it. Jordan equalized at 50' despite Austria dominating. Lost 0-2 to Argentina in MD2 despite being a legitimate quality side.
- **Pressing creates risk:** The gegenpressing system by design creates space in behind the defensive line. Against teams with pace on the counter (Messi/Argentina, Alvarez), this space is lethal. The system that creates their attack also creates their defensive vulnerability.
- **Khusanov yellow card risk:** When facing Austria's wide pressing game, their fullbacks/CBs get exposed vs pace. Khusanov was booked vs Colombia — one more yellow = suspended.

- **Over/Under tendency:** Matches tend to be lower-scoring than expected given Austria's pressing style — because the press also creates open space for opponents when it fails. Games vs better opposition (Argentina) become conservative.
- **BTTS tendency:** Austria conceded vs Jordan (equalized from open play at 50'). Their pressing creates open space. BTTS Yes is live when Austria face teams with counter-attack pace.
