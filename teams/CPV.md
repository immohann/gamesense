# 🇨🇻 CAPE VERDE
**FIFA Ranking:** 67th | **Coach:** Bubista | **System:** 4-5-1 (defensive) / 4-3-3 (when attacking) | **Group:** H

---

## Identity & DNA

- **Playing style:** Defensive, organized, compact. Deep block with 4-5-1. Low block, two banks of five, compress central space, force wide play, deny through-balls. Kevin Lenini screens the backline expertly. This is a team built entirely around defensive structure first.
- **The Vozinha factor (THE most important variable):** Goalkeeper Josimar Vozinha is 40 years old and the single most determinative factor in Cape Verde's results. Made 7 saves vs Spain, preventing 1.45 goals above expectation. Made further saves vs Uruguay. When Vozinha is in heroic form, Cape Verde can hold anyone. When he has a normal game, they concede more freely and results change dramatically. His performance variance is enormous — biggest wildcard in the tournament.
- **Opponent style sensitivity (CONFIRMED CRITICAL INSIGHT):** Cape Verde's attacking output is completely opponent-style dependent. Against possession teams (Spain): they sit even deeper, have almost zero forward momentum, 1 shot on target in 90 minutes. Against high-press/direct teams (Uruguay): they actually find more counter-attack opportunities and are genuinely dangerous going forward, scoring twice. This is the single most important insight for predicting Cape Verde games — identify opponent style first, everything else follows.
- **Under pressure:** Showed extraordinary composure vs Spain — the biggest ever ranking gap in a World Cup non-loss (2nd vs 67th). Then drew 2-2 with Uruguay despite Bielsa's high press. Massive psychological confidence and team unity. These results are not flukes.
- **Attacking:** Counter-attack only. Garry Rodrigues and Jovane Cabral / Dailon Livramento are their pace threats on the break. Their attacking threat is almost entirely dependent on the opponent leaving space on the counter.

## Key Players

- **Vozinha (Josimar Vozinha)** — Goalkeeper, 40 years old — THE key variable. National hero. 7 saves vs Spain (1.45 xGA above expectation). Made vital saves vs Uruguay too. His performance level single-handedly determines Cape Verde's results. If he has a normal game instead of a heroic one, Cape Verde concede significantly more. Always factor his likely save rate when predicting totals.

- **Diney Borges / Roberto Lopes** — Centre Backs — Physical, organized under Bubista's defensive system.

## Betting Patterns

- **Over/Under tendency:** Under vs possession teams who don't press high (see Spain 0-0). More open vs pressing/direct teams (see Uruguay 2-2). Context is everything — never apply a blanket Over or Under without identifying opponent style first.
- **BTTS tendency:** No vs possession teams (Spain clean sheet). More likely Yes vs pressing teams who leave transition space (Uruguay 2-2). Same opponent style logic applies.
- **First half:** Always cautious start regardless of opponent. Bubista will not commit forward early — the first half template is always defensive and compact. Expect Under 0.5 or 1.5 in first halves.
- **Scorer watch:** Rodrigues or Livramento on the counter when the opponent leaves space behind their defensive line. Only route to scoring — no set-piece threat, no open-play combinations.
- **⚠️ KEY WATCH #1 — Vozinha performance:** The single most important pre-match question. Is there any indication of form dip, injury concern, or normal variance? A normal Vozinha game = more goals. A heroic Vozinha game = low goals and possibly a draw regardless of quality gap.
- **⚠️ KEY WATCH #2 — Opponent style:** Possession team approaching this? Under + BTTS No. Pressing/direct team? Over + BTTS Yes. This interaction has now been confirmed across two consecutive World Cup matches and is the framework's most reliable insight for Cape Verde.
